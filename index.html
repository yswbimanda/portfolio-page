Iowa Liquor Sales Analysis

Revenue Decline Diagnosis & 2022 Growth Strategy

Iowa's alcohol tax authority needed to understand why January 2022 revenue collapsed by 34.7% ($14.7M) relative to December — and more importantly, where to focus to hit a 15% income growth target for the year. Rather than describing the drop, this project diagnosed it: identifying which counties, product categories, price tiers, and bottle sizes drove the shortfall, and building a prioritized action framework the division could operationalize.

What the data showed

49% of statewide revenue from just 5 counties
$4M+ lost from Vodka & Whiskey alone
90%+ of shortfall from Value & Premium tiers
80% of lost sales in Medium & Extra Large bottles

Project Outputs

Sales Overview Dashboard

This dashboard provides a full picture of Iowa liquor sales performance across the analysis period. The January 2022 revenue of $27.6M sits 34.7% below December 2021's $42.3M — a drop that looks seasonal on the surface but reveals structural concentration risks when drilled down by geography, category, and price tier. The benchmark line from March–September 2021 confirms this isn't a normal seasonal dip.

County Revenue Gap Analysis

Iowa's revenue is not distributed evenly — just 5 counties (Polk, Linn, Scott, Johnson, and Black Hawk) account for 49% of statewide sales. All five underperformed against the benchmark in January 2022. This concentration means that operational gaps in a small number of markets create disproportionate statewide impact — and that recovery in these counties alone can meaningfully move the overall number.

Category Family Performance

Vodka and Whiskey together account for over $4M of the January shortfall — making them the highest-priority recovery categories. These aren't niche products; they are the core of consumer demand in Iowa's liquor market. The analysis shows that broad category-level promotion won't be enough — SKU-level targeting within these two families is where the recovery opportunity sits.

Price Tier & Size Segment Analysis

The Value ($10–21) and Premium ($22–75) tiers contributed over 90% of the revenue shortfall — confirming this is a mainstream consumer behavior shift, not a luxury market problem. Medium and Extra Large bottle sizes account for nearly 80% of lost sales volume, pointing to bulk and regular-use purchasing patterns being disrupted. Inventory and pricing alignment in these two segments is the highest-leverage operational fix available.

Recommendations

Critical

Build demand forecasting for Value & Premium price bands — supply misalignment here creates outsized statewide impact.

High Priority

Implement monthly variance dashboards for Polk, Linn, Scott, Johnson & Black Hawk — early signals in these 5 counties predict statewide performance.

High Priority

SKU-level audit inside Vodka & Whiskey families to find specific lagging labels — broad category recovery starts with brand-level targeting.
